  • Background in this Subject:None.
  • Courses I’m in and Why:
    1. MUSC1231L - Aural Skills & Sight-Singing ll: Music education majors have to learn a lot of different skills. Some of these skills include aural skills and sight-singing. Aural skills are also known as “ear training,” which is essential in developing the ability to hear and interpret rhythm, melodies, triads, and more. Sight-singing helps improve sight-reading skills. If you can sing it, then you can play it.
    2. MUSC1234 - Class Piano ll: I have to learn how to play the piano. The piano is a universal instrument that can be useful when teaching in the future. Additionally, it helps with learning theory.
    3. MUSC1231 - Music Theory: Theory can help improve one's ability to read music in general. In the future, my students need a basic understanding of theory in order to play repertoire.
    4. MUSC1300 - Performance Class: This allows music majors to practice performing in front of others and prepare for Juries or Recitals.
    5. MUSC1229 - Percussion Techniques: I have to learn all instruments which include percussion, strings, and brass.
    6. MUED2100 - Intro to Music Education: This course is an introduction to how to teach.
    7. MUSC1502 - Global Arts/Humanities: Music in Global Communities: I needed this course to finish my general education.
    8. ITCS-1110 - Computer Science Principles: I needed this course to finish my general education.
    9. MUPF1112 - Wind Ensemble: As a music major, I am required to be in an ensemble.
    10. MUPF1114 - Basketball Band: I am the basketball band for a scholarship and to support our Charlotte 49ers Basketball Teams!
    11. MUPF1244 - Applied Music-Tuba: These are lessons for my primary instrument. I am required to take this class for all four years.
